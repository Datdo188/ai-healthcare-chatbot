Meta description:
Sore throat or pharyngitis has many causes including allergies, smoking, strep throat, and mononucleosis. Find treatments options depending on cause.

Also called:
- Pharyngitis

Groups:
- Ear, Nose and Throat

Summary:
<p>Your throat is a tube that carries food to your esophagus and air to your windpipe and larynx (also called the voice box). The technical name for the throat is pharynx.</p>

<p>You can have a sore throat for many reasons. Often, <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/commoncold.html">colds</a> and <a href="https://medlineplus.gov/flu.html">flu</a> cause sore throats. Other causes can include:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/allergy.html">Allergies</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/infectiousmononucleosis.html">Mononucleosis</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/smoking.html">Smoking</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/streptococcalinfections.html">Strep throat</a></li>
<li><a href="https://medlineplus.gov/tonsillitis.html">Tonsillitis</a></li>
</ul>

<p>Treatment depends on the cause. Sucking on lozenges, drinking lots of liquids, and gargling may ease the pain. Over-the-counter pain relievers can also help, but children should not take aspirin.</p>
